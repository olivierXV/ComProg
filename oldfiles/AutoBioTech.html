Career and Linux

- The first prototypes of Linux was release in 1991 while version 1.0 was release in March 14, 1994. Linus first encountered the GNU Project in 1991, he would ultimately switch to GNU GPLv2 for his Linux Project. In late 1996, he accepted a position at the company in California, where he worked from February 1997 to June 2003. He then moved to the Open Source Development Labs, which has since merged with the Free Standards Group to become the Linux Foundation. And from 1997 to 1999, he was involved in 86open, helping select the standard binary format for Linux and Unix.

Personal Life

- Linus Torvalds is currently Married Tove Torvalds, a six-time Finnish national karate champion, they have two daughters. In 2004, he and his family moved from Silicon Valley to Portland, Oregon, and in 2010, he became a US Citizen.
